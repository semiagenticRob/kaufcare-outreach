# Ground-Level Activation Strategies — 2515 Eliot St, Denver 80211

**Prepared:** June 2026  
**Clinic:** 2515 Eliot St, Denver, CO 80211 (LoHi / Jefferson Park)  
**Target radius:** ½ mile — 16 apartment complexes, ~2,000 units, ~5,000 new residents/year with no local doctor

---

## Strategy A: Leasing Office Welcome Packet Partnerships

Embed the clinic into the move-in experience at the 16 apartment complexes. Every new lease signed = a warm introduction before the resident even needs care.

- Approach property managers (starting with Cornerstone, who manages 5 buildings from one office) and propose a co-branded welcome card or small kit (clinic intro, transparent pricing one-pager, first-visit incentive) placed in every new move-in folder
- Offer something of value back to the leasing office — a "resident perk" they can advertise in listings (e.g., "complimentary new-resident health consultation for all tenants"), which helps them differentiate in a market where buildings are competing with 8-12 weeks of free rent
- Prioritize Skyline (533 units, active resident programming by manager Megan), Alton Jefferson Park (122 units, already hosts community events), and Decatur Point (203 units, strong community manager Greg) as first three pitches
- Refresh materials seasonally so the relationship stays active and doesn't become stale collateral sitting in a drawer
- Goal: become the default healthcare recommendation that every leasing agent gives when a new resident asks "know a good doctor nearby?"

---

## Strategy B: Apartment Community Event Co-Sponsorship

Show up inside the buildings where residents already gather. Multiple complexes in your radius already run resident events — attach the clinic's name and presence to those moments.

- Skyline, Alton, Alcott, Decatur Point, and Infinity all run regular resident events (rooftop happy hours, holiday parties, potlucks, pumpkin painting). Propose co-sponsoring one event per quarter at each building — bring branded supplies, a simple health resource, and a face from the clinic
- Keep it lifestyle-aligned, not clinical: blood pressure checks at a rooftop happy hour, a hydration station at a summer pool event, a "flu season prep" table at a fall gathering — low friction, high memorability
- Use these as face-to-face relationship builders with building staff and residents simultaneously; the leasing team sees you as a partner, the residents meet a real person from the clinic
- Track which buildings convert to visits and double down on those relationships

---

## Strategy C: Neighborhood Business Cross-Referral Network

LoHi residents discover services through the businesses they already frequent daily. Become part of the local merchant ecosystem rather than marketing in isolation.

- Identify the 8-10 highest-foot-traffic businesses within walking distance of the clinic: coffee shops (Starbucks at Safeway, local cafes), breweries (Little Machine Beer, Briar Common), fitness studios/gyms, dog grooming, the Safeway community board, and coworking spaces (Industrious, Switchyards, Strive)
- Propose simple reciprocal arrangements: clinic flyers or cards at their counter, their cards at your front desk; co-branded "neighborhood guide" for new residents that features both businesses
- Target coworking spaces specifically — Industrious has two LoHi locations filled with remote workers and freelancers who likely lack employer-provided healthcare navigation and would value a transparent, walk-in-friendly clinic nearby
- Host or co-host a quarterly "neighborhood business mixer" that positions the clinic as a community anchor, not just a transactional service provider

---

## Strategy D: Direct Door-to-Door Canvassing and Door Hanger Drops

The most labor-intensive but highest-signal activation. Physically show up at every door in your half-mile radius with a clear, concise message.

- Design a single, high-quality door hanger (not a flyer — it hangs on the knob and can't be ignored) with three things only: what you are, where you are, and one reason to visit (transparent pricing, no-wait policy, or a first-visit offer)
- Execute in waves by building: start with the inner ring (2525 Eliot, Decatur Point, Alton, Jefferson Park 33, Edge25) where you're a literal neighbor, then expand to the outer ring
- For buildings with secure entry where you can't access individual doors, this reverts to the leasing office strategy — leave a stack with the front desk and ask for placement in mail rooms or common areas
- Pair the drop with a personal introduction to building management on the same visit; the door hanger gets you in front of residents, the handshake gets you in front of the gatekeeper
- Repeat every 4-6 months to catch the new cohort (27% annual turnover means a quarter of your audience is new each cycle)

---

## Strategy E: Pop-Up Presence at Neighborhood Gathering Points

Meet residents where they already spend weekend time — the farmer's market, the parks, the dog-walking circuits, and gameday foot traffic near Empower Field.

- The Highland Square Farmers Market (Sundays, 32nd & Lowell) draws heavy foot traffic from exactly your target demographic; secure a small booth or table presence with a simple "meet your neighborhood clinic" setup
- Jefferson Park, Hirshorn Park, and the Cuernavaca Park trail along the South Platte are daily dog-walking corridors — a branded pop-up with dog treats and a clinic card is a low-cost, high-touch activation (this is an extremely dog-friendly zip code; nearly every building on your list advertises pet amenities)
- Broncos gamedays bring tens of thousands past your doorstep; a simple sidewalk presence (table, banner, hand-out) on the walk from parking to Empower Field captures eyeballs from both residents and visitors
- Rotate locations to avoid fatigue and test which touchpoints convert — track with a simple "how did you hear about us?" at intake
